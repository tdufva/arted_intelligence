Corpus Visualization

Studies in Art Education

How the journal thinks intelligence.

A corpus map of the articles in the All Intelligence article in Studies folder, showing how intelligence is defined, recognized, located, and distributed across subjects, alongside the broader shifts from developmental measurement toward more social, ecological, and digital framings.

Data feminism in practice

The page avoids a single hierarchy of intelligence. Articles can belong to multiple categories, context is visible, interpretive labor is shown, and critical, disability, community, and technological perspectives are surfaced alongside classical cognition.

Source Boundary

All trend analysis, coding, search, quotes, validation, and question answering on this page are built from the provided PDF corpus. The only outside data shown publicly is an optional citation snapshot, isolated in the external tab in section 13.

    How this page was built

    Design commitments

    Analysis and evidence on this page come from the provided PDFs. Outside references are kept separate here as design guidance or, in section 13, as an optional external citation layer.

    • Data Feminism informed the page through plural coding, contextual framing, and visible methodological labor.
    • Datawrapper’s guidance on labels shaped the use of direct labels, clear section copy, and minimal axis clutter.
    • Datawrapper’s color guidance and Crameri, Shephard, and Heron (2020) informed the contrast-first, color-vision-aware palette.
    • OpenAlex provided the external citation snapshot used in the “most-cited” section.

    Caveat: article coding is heuristic and multi-label. It is designed to reveal patterns for exploration, not to replace close reading.

    This public version is intentionally evidence-rich but export-light: it shows article-level evidence and the codebook on the page, but does not offer a downloadable corpus dataset.
