How the ranking works

              

                

                  
Observed overlap

                  
The number of articles carrying both the primary concept and the crossreference concept inside the selected scope.

                

                

                  
Lift

                  
The observed overlap divided by the overlap expected if the two concepts were independent in the current scope.

                

                

                  
Frontier signal

                  
The change in pairing share between the early and late corpus windows, using the selected primary concept as the denominator.

                

                

                  
Evidence first

                  
Every lead stays tied to article-level quotes and coding confidence so pattern finding does not outrun the source material.

                

              

            

            

              
Interpretive cautions

              

                

                  
Multi-label corpus

                  
Articles can carry multiple codes at once, so intersections show co-presence, not exclusive classification.

                

                

                  
Small-N edges

                  
Rare pairings can be genuinely interesting, but they should be treated as prompts for closer reading rather than settled trends.

                

                

                  
Citation caution

                  
PDF citation metadata is partial in this corpus, so citation-dense leads are suggestive rather than a complete impact measure.

                

                

                  
Data feminism principle

                  
The page tries to reward marginal yet meaningful strands, not just majoritarian centers of the field.
